Job tasks on home weeks go in the interruptible morning slot — not deep work. Deep work is protected in both modes. If a good conversation is actively in play, that slot expands slightly; the app session shrinks but doesn't disappear.
  • 🛡
    Mornings are protected by default. A recruiter email at 9am gets a reply at 10:30 or noon. Nothing is so urgent it can't wait 90 minutes.
  • ⏱
    2 hours/day is the ceiling for job prep, research, and follow-up — unless you consciously call a surge day.
  • 🔥
    Surge days are real, but named. Big presentation due? Take-home exercise? Say out loud: "this is a surge day." One or two per active conversation. Not the whole duration.
  • 📉
    The crash is proportional to the sprint. Consistent moderate effort compounds. Drop-everything mode sets you up for a harder landing when it doesn't pan out.
  • 🔄
    The app keeps running. Even 30 minutes holds the thread during an active hiring process. Don't let a conversation erase the other thing that's building.
  • ✅
    You have more in place now. Portfolio done. Stories rehearsed. Website live. Prep is refinement, not reconstruction — hours, not days.
The reframe in one sentence: a new conversation is a good thing that fits into your life — not a thing your life reorganizes around.
